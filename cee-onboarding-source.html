How to Open a Case

Three ways to reach us
Option A — Portal Recommended

Go to access.redhat.com/support/cases → Open a New Case

Fastest routing. Full case history. Attach files directly.

Option B — Phone

Call your regional support hotline. For Sev 1, open online first then follow up by phone with your case number.

Option C — Chat (English)

Live chat available via the Customer Portal in English. Good for quick questions.

Case Details

What to include for fast resolution

Providing thorough details upfront is the single biggest factor in faster case resolution.

Environment Details

FieldExamples
Platform versionRHEL 9.2, OpenShift 4.14, Satellite 6.12
Product versionJBoss EAP 7.4, Ansible Automation Platform 2.4
Third-party productsAny vendor software involved in the issue

Issue Details

FieldWhat to provide
TimestampsWhen the issue first appeared and any recurrence pattern
Error messagesExact text — copy/paste, not paraphrased
Steps to reproduceA numbered sequence that reliably triggers the issue
Multi-vendor detailsVendor name, their case number, and vendor contact email if applicable

Attachments

Recommended Files

SOSreport — system state snapshot for RHEL

vmcore — kernel crash dump (required for panic RCA)

Log files — application and system logs around the incident window

Uploading Large Files

Attach directly to your case on the portal, or use the Red Hat FTP site: access.redhat.com/solutions/2112

Respond promptly to any follow-up requests from your support engineer — additional details are often needed to avoid delays.

Severity Levels

Choosing the right priority
Important: Choosing a severity level higher than appropriate may delay routing to the right team. Match your severity to the actual business impact.
SeverityLabelDescription
1 — UrgentComplete outageProduction systems not functioning. No workaround available. Full service impact.
2 — HighProduction severely degradedHigh business impact. No workaround. Key feature unavailable.
3 — MediumNo critical lossMedium to low impact in production or development. Workaround may exist.
4 — LowGeneral / DocumentationMinimal business impact. How-to questions, doc errors, feature requests.

Managed Services

ARO & ROSA

If you are running a managed OpenShift environment on a major cloud provider, your support model is a joint responsibility between Red Hat and the cloud provider.

Azure Red Hat OpenShift (ARO)

What is ARO?
A jointly managed OpenShift cluster on Azure, operated by Microsoft and Red Hat together
SLA & Severity Levels
Severity levels align with Azure and Red Hat policies; review the Service Level Agreement
Case Handling
Cases may be filed on either the Azure portal or the Red Hat Customer Portal; teams coordinate internally

Red Hat OpenShift Service on AWS (ROSA)

ROSA Lifecycle
Full cluster lifecycle support — from provisioning through decommissioning
Support Process
Open cases via the Red Hat Customer Portal; Red Hat and AWS coordinate for platform-level issues
Escalation Best Practices
Document impact clearly and follow the standard escalation path when SLAs are at risk

Escalation

When and how to escalate

If your issue is not progressing as expected, or the business impact has increased, you can escalate at any time — escalation is a feature, not a failure.

SignalAction
No meaningful update after a reasonable period at current severityRequest escalation in the case comments
Business impact has grown since case was openedUpdate severity level and notify your support engineer
SLA breach is imminent or has occurredContact support engineer directly or use the escalation path
Multiple teams need to coordinate and progress has stalledAsk for a cross-team escalation meeting

Management Escalation

Step 1 — In the Portal

Open your case and click "Request Management Escalation". This immediately flags the case for management attention.

Step 2 — Follow Up by Phone

Call your regional support hotline and ask to speak to a Delivery Manager.

Reference: access.redhat.com/support/policy/mgt_escalation
