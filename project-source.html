01 · Problem Statement

There is no single source of truth for CEE

CEE has no single authoritative resource explaining how customers engage with Red Hat Support. What exists today is a collection of independently created decks, high-level XE content, LMS courses, KB articles, and team-specific guides — maintained by different owners, stored in different places, and updated on no consistent schedule. Some resources contain outdated branding. Some are scoped only to a specific team or region. None of them are owned end-to-end by CEE.

This means that incorrect or outdated information is being used, which leads to inconsistent experiences for customers.

"What do I send them?" — there is currently no definitive answer to this question for a new customer or a new team member.
02 · Why Now

Three forces making this urgent

01
Customer onboarding matters
Customer onboarding is the moment a subscription becomes real for a customer. Done well, it sets expectations, surfaces value early, and prevents the confusion that drives churn. Done poorly — or left to chance — customers disengage before they ever get started.

For AI-powered tools like Ask Red Hat to work without hallucination, the content they draw from has to be accurate, current, and in one place. That starts here.
02
Missed opportunities in Marketing touchpoint
Marketing is already in the customer's inbox at the most critical moment — day one. The marketing team is actively sending onboarding emails to customers. If CEE's knowledge is not connected to that program, we are missing the highest-value touchpoint in the customer lifecycle.
03
H.E.A.R.T. Vision
The CEE H.E.A.R.T. vision puts a human touch at the center of the customer experience. A live agent, personalized onboarding, proactive engagement — all of that is possible. But every one of those programs needs a single, trusted source of content underneath it. This is how we build the foundation.
03 · Project Objective

Create and maintain a single CEE-owned onboarding resource

The CEE Goldendeck is the authoritative source of truth for how Red Hat Support works — for both customers and internal teams. It is a living document with named section owners, a quarterly review cycle, and a defined process for its knowledge to reach the Customer Portal and marketing programs.

04 · Scope

What this covers

External — Customer-facing
How to engage support, open cases, choose severity, run diagnostics, escalate, and navigate managed services (ARO/ROSA).
Internal — Team-facing
CEE org structure, team routing, Slack contacts, MCS workflows, and the content pipeline from CEE to the Customer Portal.
Dedicated home · Multiple formats
A single permanent location for CEE onboarding content that branches into whatever format is needed — interactive web guide, Source page embed, PDF, email copy, and eventual Ask Red Hat integration and marketing content feeds.
05 · Governance Model

How it stays current

Step 1
Name an owner
Each CEE team nominates one person to own their section
Quarterly
Review & flag
Owner reads their section and gives an ack or nack on accuracy
Quarterly
Portal brief
Owner writes a short brief for the Portal team on what needs updating
As needed
Marketing sync
Changes affecting customers are flagged to the marketing team
06 · Definition of Done — v1

What success looks like

  • Named owner confirmed for each CEE team section
  • All sections reviewed and approved by respective owners
  • Dedicated place established — live on Customer Portal
  • Quarterly review cadence established with owners
