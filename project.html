01 · Problem Statement

There is no single source of truth for CEE

CEE has no single authoritative resource explaining how customers engage with Red Hat Support. What exists today is a collection of independently created decks, high-level XE content, LMS courses, KB articles, and team-specific guides — maintained by different owners, stored in different places, and updated on no consistent schedule.

Some resources contain outdated branding. Some are scoped only to a specific team or region. None of them are owned end-to-end by CEE.

"What do I send them?" — there is currently no definitive answer to this question for a new customer or a new team member.
02 · Why Now

Three forces making this urgent

01
Marketing touchpoint
The marketing team is actively sending onboarding emails to customers. If CEE's knowledge is not connected to that program, we are missing the highest-value touchpoint in the customer lifecycle.
02
Portal gap
The Customer Portal team owns the public-facing support pages. CEE is the subject matter expert. There is currently no formal process for CEE knowledge to flow to those pages.
03
Aging resources
The existing resources are aging. Some still display the retired Shadowman logo. Without an ownership model, the gap between what is published and what is accurate will only grow.
03 · Project Objective

Create and maintain a single CEE-owned onboarding resource

The CEE Goldendeck is the authoritative source of truth for how Red Hat Support works — for both customers and internal teams. It is a living document with named section owners, a quarterly review cycle, and a defined process for its knowledge to reach the Customer Portal and marketing programs.

04 · Scope

What this covers

External view — Customer-facing
How to engage support, open cases, choose severity, run diagnostics, escalate, and navigate managed services (ARO/ROSA). Designed for customers at the start of their Red Hat journey.
Internal view — Team-facing
CEE org structure, team routing, Slack contacts, MCS workflows, and the content pipeline from CEE to the Customer Portal. Designed for new and existing CEE team members.
Dedicated home · Multiple formats
A single, permanent location for CEE onboarding content that branches into whatever format is needed — interactive web guide, Source page embed, PDF handout, email copy, and eventual Ask Red Hat integration and marketing content feeds. Content is written once and maintained in one place.
05 · Governance Model

How it stays current

Step 1
Name an owner
Each CEE team nominates one person to own their section of the guide
Quarterly
Review & flag
Owner reads their section, flags anything inaccurate or missing, gives an ack or nack
Quarterly
Portal brief
Owner writes a short brief for the Portal team specifying what changed and what page needs updating
As needed
Marketing sync
Changes that affect customer-facing content are flagged to the marketing team for email program updates

CEE owns the knowledge. The Portal team publishes it. Marketing distributes it. The Goldendeck is the pipe between them.

06 · Definition of Done — v1

What success looks like

  • Named owner confirmed for each CEE team section
  • All sections reviewed and approved by respective owners
  • Dedicated place established — live on GitHub Pages, embedded on Source
  • Quarterly review cadence established with owners
